Frequently Asked Questions

When should I hire a real estate attorney?

It's best to involve a real estate attorney at the beginning of any property transaction to ensure your interests are protected from the start. We can review contracts, conduct due diligence, and identify potential issues before they become problems.

What's the difference between a title search and title insurance?

A title search examines public records to verify the property's ownership history and identify any liens or claims. Title insurance protects against financial loss from defects in title that weren't discovered during the title search.

How long does a typical real estate closing take?

While timelines can vary, most residential real estate transactions close within 30-45 days from the acceptance of the purchase agreement. Commercial transactions may take longer depending on the complexity of the deal.

What should I look for in a commercial lease?

Key considerations include lease term, rent escalation clauses, maintenance responsibilities, assignment and subletting provisions, renewal options, and exit strategies. Having an attorney review the lease can help identify potential issues and negotiate better terms.
