Frequently Asked Questions

What is a class action lawsuit?

A class action is a type of lawsuit where one or several plaintiffs sue on behalf of a larger group of people who have similar claims against the same defendant.

How does a case become a class action?

For a case to become a class action, the court must certify the class by finding that the case meets specific legal requirements, including numerosity, commonality, typicality, and adequacy of representation.

What are the potential damages in a class action?

Damages in class actions can be substantial, often reaching millions or even billions of dollars, making it crucial to mount an effective defense early in the litigation.

How long does class action litigation typically last?

Class actions can last anywhere from several months to several years, depending on the complexity of the case, the court's schedule, and whether the case goes to trial or is resolved through settlement.

What should I do if my business is served with a class action complaint?

Contact experienced class action defense counsel immediately. The initial stages of a class action are critical, and prompt action can significantly impact the outcome of the case.
