Frequently Asked Questions

What is a qui tam lawsuit?

A qui tam lawsuit is a legal action brought by a private individual (the "relator") on behalf of the government to recover funds obtained through fraud against government programs. Successful whistleblowers may receive a portion of the recovered funds.

How much can a whistleblower receive?

Under the False Claims Act, whistleblowers can receive between 15-30% of the government's recovery, depending on various factors. Some cases have resulted in awards of millions of dollars to whistleblowers.

Will my identity be protected?

Yes, whistleblower complaints are filed under seal, meaning they are kept confidential while the government investigates. Your identity remains protected throughout this process.

What protections do I have against retaliation?

Federal and state laws protect whistleblowers from workplace retaliation, including termination, demotion, or harassment. If you experience retaliation, you may be entitled to reinstatement, back pay, and other damages.
